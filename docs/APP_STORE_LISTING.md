
Deliberately omits "perfect pitch": the app does not train it, and the term
invites 1-star "this isn't perfect pitch" reviews. "relative pitch" used instead.
"daily puzzle" added for the Daily mode; "do re mi" dropped (covered by the app
name + "solfege"). No "Wordle" — trademarked; describe the daily as a shared puzzle.

## Description (≤4000)

```
Ear training that feels like play, built on a method that works.

DoReMingo helps you recognise musical notes by ear — no sheet music, no theory degree, no "you either have it or you don't." Just a cheerful little game and a flamingo who's genuinely glad you showed up.

It's built on the Kodály method — the same approach used to teach music in schools around the world — reshaped into something you'll actually look forward to opening.

How it works
A few chords set the key in your ear. A note plays. You tap which one it was. Right or wrong, you hear the note gently walk home to Do — the little "aha" that teaches your ear what each note feels like. That walk home is the whole trick, and it's why it sticks.

Three ways to play
• Learn — twelve levels that grow with you, from three friendly notes to full scales, colour notes, minor keys and short melodies. You're never thrown in the deep end.
• Daily — one shared puzzle for everyone, fresh every day. Hear a hidden little tune, echo it back note by note, and share your spoiler-free grid. One a day.
• Warmup — a quick, no-stakes ear-tune for when you've got a spare minute. It meets you where you are and keeps your ear sharp.

Why it's different
• No fail states. Get one wrong and nothing bad happens — you just keep going. The progress bar nudges forward, never punishes.
• Bright, calm, unhurried. No timers breathing down your neck, no pressure.
• A flamingo. We won't oversell this part. He's just nice to have around.

Works fully offline, saves your progress, and it's free. Pop in for two minutes or twenty.

Come train your ear the easy way. 🦩
```

## Category

- **Primary: Education**
- **Secondary: Music**

Education primary: it's a learning tool (Kodály ear training) for the curious
adult beginner, not entertainment/playback. Education has winnable competition
(Music top-charts = Spotify/Shazam/GarageBand) and matches search intent (people
wanting to *learn* music). Music kept as secondary for music-curious browsers and
keyword relevance.

## Decisions (from grilling)

- **Audience:** curious adult beginner (hero); teachers/parents = keyword play only.
- **Hook:** proven method + zero stress/boredom (credibility + ease).
- **Method naming:** Kodály named once, glossed in plain language; no further jargon.
- **Three modes:** Learn (the 12-level spine), Daily (shared once-a-day puzzle, the
  social/retention hook), Warmup (transient quick drill). Listed as "Three ways to
  play" so the daily and the quick-minute use cases are both discoverable up front.
- **Feature heroes:** no-fail, walk-home Resolution, gentle 12-level ramp, flamingo cheer.
  Offline/free/saves = one supporting line.
- **Daily naming:** never "Wordle" (trademarked); "one shared puzzle for everyone,
  fresh every day" + "spoiler-free grid" conveys the format without the brand.
- **Tone ceiling:** warm but grounded — restrained, one flamingo wink, sparse emoji,
  no exclamation pileups. Avoids the "kids app" read for an adult beginner.
