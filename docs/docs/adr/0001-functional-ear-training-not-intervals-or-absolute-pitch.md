# Functional ear training, not intervals or absolute pitch

The game's premise is "guess the note", but we deliberately do not train note names (absolute pitch is near-untrainable in adults) nor interval drills (the traditional conservatory approach — established but weakly transferable because intervals are heard out of tonal context). We train scale-degree recognition within a key established by a cadence (functional ear training, Kodály movable-do tradition, popularised by Alain Benbassat's method). Every downstream design follows from this: answers are solfège degrees not note names, every question carries tonal context, and the stepwise resolution to Do after each answer is the teaching mechanism itself.

## Consequences

- Note names (C, D, F#) must never appear as answer options.
- Audio must always establish key before questions (cadence), except at levels that deliberately withhold it as a difficulty step.
- Pitch content of questions is meaningless without the preceding cadence — questions cannot be generated or replayed in isolation.
